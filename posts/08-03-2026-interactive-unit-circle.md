# I built an interactive unit circle

I got tired of memorising trigonometry by rote. What's the range of cosine? What's the sign of tangent in the third quadrant? I could drill flashcards, sure, but none of it ever stuck — because I wasn't building any real intuition.

So I built an [interactive unit circle](/unit-circle.html). Drag a point around the circle and watch sine, cosine and tangent update in real time. You can toggle each function on and off and see exactly how they relate to the angle and the geometry of the circle.

Once you _see_ that sine is just the y-coordinate, you stop memorising its range — you just know it. Same with the signs of each function in each quadrant. It becomes obvious rather than something you recall.

I've found this pattern over and over in my maths journey: the things that feel hardest to memorise are usually the things I don't yet understand visually. Building small tools like this is my antidote to rote learning.
