# How much time I've spent on Math Academy

A while ago I wrote that [maths is now the best part of my day](/maths-is-now-the-best-part-of-my-day.html). Since then, the question I get most often is some version of: "Okay, but how much time does it actually take?"

[Math Academy](https://www.mathacademy.com/) logs everything, so I exported my activity log and had a look. Here's the honest answer.

I started Mathematical Foundations I on 2 February 2024. Since then I've shown up on 583 days — roughly two out of every three days — and earned 14,111 XP across 1,689 tasks (737 of them lessons). Math Academy's rule of thumb is that 1 XP corresponds to about a minute of focused work, so that's in the region of 235 hours of maths.

Here's how it breaks down per course:

| Course | Status | Active days | Total XP | XP / active day | Active days / week |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Foundations I | Completed | 138 | 3,357 | 24.3 | 4.6 |
| Foundations II | Completed | 246 | 6,853 | 27.9 | 5.0 |
| Foundations III | In progress | 199 | 3,901 | 19.6 | 4.0 |

(An "active day" is any day I completed at least one task.)

Two things stand out to me.

First, the whole thing runs on a surprisingly small daily habit. Averaged out, I do about 24 XP per active day — call it 25 minutes of focused work — four to five days a week. That's it. No heroic weekends, no six-hour study sessions. My biggest day ever was 68 XP, barely more than an hour. My longest streak is 19 days, and I don't chase streaks.

Second, Foundations III is slower going, and the numbers show exactly how. I still show up about four days a week (down a little from five), but my sessions have shrunk from about 28 minutes to about 20. The material is heavier now — calculus, linear algebra, differential equations — and a session takes more out of me than it used to. So the frequency has mostly held; it's the intensity that dropped.

That, I think, is the real lesson in the data: showing up regularly matters far more than showing up impressively. Twenty-five focused minutes, most days, compounds into things I once thought were beyond me.

In my previous post I wrote that I'll "never" be finished with maths. This is what never looks like in practice: about 25 minutes, most days, indefinitely. I can happily live with that.
